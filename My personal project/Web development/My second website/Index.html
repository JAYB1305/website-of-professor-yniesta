About Me:

I grew up in France in Saint-Etienne, a city known for its coal mine and its soccer team. I copleted the first half of my bachelors degree in Loyn at the institut Universitaire de Technologie Lyon 1 (IUT A Lyon 1) and then continued my studies in Paris at the Ecole Speciale des Travaux Publics (ESTP). While completing my bachelors degree, I studied Civil Engineering with a focus on Public Works . In 2012 I obtained aMaster of Sience at UCLA IN Geomtechnical Engineering, after which I immediatlely started my Ph.D. undeer the supervision of Scott Brandenberg.

During my Ph.d, i worked on centrifuge tests of leavesatop peaty organic soil, and on constitutive modeling of soils under dynamic loading. After completing my Ph.D. in March 2016, I immediately started my position at the Ecole Polytechnique of Montréal.
